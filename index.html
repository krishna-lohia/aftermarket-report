Fear takes hold — Nifty drops 2.12% on broad, heavy selling

Markets Today

Nifty 50 fell 517 points (-2.12%) to close at 23,882.05. Sensex mirrored the move, fell 2.15% to 76,503.6. VIX jumped 26.01% to 14.7.

Bank Nifty tracked the market closely, fell 2.51% to 56,742.6.

Breadth was poor — 435 declines vs 65 advances out of 500 stocks. A 6.7:1 ratio means selling was broad, not concentrated in a few names.

Commodities

Crude oil rose 6.03% to ₹7,109 per barrel. That is something to watch closely. India imports most of its crude, so a sharp rise in oil prices feeds into higher fuel costs, widens the trade deficit, and puts pressure on the rupee.

Gold fell 1.47% to ₹1,43,250/10g; silver similarly dropped 3.01% to ₹2,23,899/kg.

The rupee weakened to 95.74 against the dollar (+0.70%). A weaker rupee makes imports costlier (especially crude oil) but is actually good for IT and pharma companies that earn in dollars — their revenues look bigger in rupee terms.

Options Activity

Options positioning: resistance at 24,500 (heaviest call OI), support at 23,500 (heaviest put OI). PCR at 0.61 is bearish — calls outnumber puts, watch for complacency.

Nifty is below both the 21-EMA (23,985.01) and 50-EMA (23,932.31) — neither the short nor medium-term trend is supportive. Getting back above 23,985.01 is the first step.

VIX rose 26.01% to 14.7. Below 15 is the calm zone. Good for bulls, though complacency can bite.
