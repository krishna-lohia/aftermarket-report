Nifty stumbles after three-day rally as banking and auto buckle — metals still lead

Market Overview

The three-day rally that had lifted Nifty past 24,200 came to a halt today. The index closed at 24,174.55, down 56 points or 0.23%, while Sensex fell 0.27%. This marks the first pullback after FIIs had steadied flows for three straight sessions. The selling wasn't aggressive, but it was enough to stall momentum. VIX eased to 18.35, reflecting contained volatility, yet breadth turned slightly positive at 4,458 advances versus 4,165 declines. The message is clear: the rally was losing steam even before today's weakness set in.

Broader Market

Banking and auto bore the brunt of the decline. PSU Bank fell 0.49%, Realty dropped 0.47%, and Auto slid 0.45%. Metals alone held ground with a 1.35% gain, while IT managed a small 0.34% rise. Midcaps, though, bucked the trend and rose 0.19%, suggesting that smaller names found value as the index stumbled. Only 2 out of 12 sectors closed green, a sharp reversal from yesterday when all 12 were positive. The rally's breadth was always suspect, and today confirmed it was frontloaded into large-caps.

The economic calendar from yesterday showed WPI inflation holding at 2.13%, unemployment at 4.9%, and bank credit growing 13.85% year-on-year, all baseline numbers that don't scream crisis. Without major global catalysts today, the focus remains on how domestic momentum sustains from here.

Institutional Flows

FIIs continued to buy, adding 666 Cr today, matching their inflow from two days ago. This consistency is worth watching because it suggests they're not spooked by the pullback. DIIs, on the other hand, turned net sellers of 569 Cr, giving back some of their defensive positioning. The rupee strengthened 1.48%, closing at 92.12, which typically supports foreign inflows but can dampen export earnings. Gold rose 0.47% while crude dipped 0.64%, with the stronger rupee likely tempering energy prices. Silver outpaced gold with a 0.83% gain, hinting at some cyclical optimism beneath the surface weakness.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

The rally broke, but the fall wasn't dramatic. Banking weakness is the real concern here, and until that sector finds its feet, the broader index will struggle to build conviction above 24,300. Metals are keeping the ship afloat, but relying on one sector to drive a 12-sector index is fragile. Watch how Nifty trades around 24,100-24,300 tomorrow. A hold above 24,100 keeps the uptrend alive; a break below it reopens the door to 23,800.
