Banking's weight pins Nifty as metals and IT keep the floor intact

Market Overview

Nifty finished marginally lower at 24,159.95, giving back a fraction of yesterday's bounce. The index couldn't sustain momentum as banking and auto stocks rolled over, though metals and IT held up to prevent a sharper decline. Breadth tilted just barely positive with 4,408 advances against 4,211 declines, suggesting the selloff lacked conviction. VIX dipped to 18.47, sitting comfortably in the low-volatility zone after three sessions of mostly calm conditions.

Broader Market

Midcap managed a tiny gain while the broader index wobbled, a dynamic that's become familiar over the past few days. Among the 12 sectors tracked, only metals and IT managed green finishes, with metals jumping 1.48% on continued strength in precious metals (silver up 0.89%, gold up 0.55%). Banking and PSU banks both slipped 0.47% and 0.53% respectively, signaling that the recovery story from Tuesday hasn't found its legs yet. Auto's 0.51% drop added to the pain, making financials and cyclicals the clear drag on the day.

Institutional Flows

FIIs stayed put with another ₹666 Cr inflow, marking their second consecutive day of buying at these levels. It's worth watching because their rhythm over the past week has been choppy (₹-2,812 Cr, ₹-1,711 Cr, then back to positive), yet they've steadied here. DIIs turned net sellers to the tune of ₹569 Cr, cashing in some gains after a couple of days of buying. The rupee strengthened meaningfully, gaining 1.48%, while crude slipped 0.6%, both moves that typically carry signal about capital flows and sentiment. With the economic calendar having delivered WPI, employment, and credit data yesterday, today felt like consolidation rather than reaction.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

Two sessions of recovery followed by two of backing and filling. The pattern isn't alarming, but it does suggest the index is searching for a fresh catalyst to break out from the 24,000-24,200 band. Banks held up the worst, but metals and IT's resilience kept today from turning into another broad selloff. Investors holding ground here are betting that Friday brings either clarity or a push higher; a drift below 24,100 would invite more defensive positioning.
