Commodities

Crude oil fell 12.09% to ₹7,785 per barrel. That is good news for India, which imports around 85% of its crude oil requirements. Cheaper oil reduces the import bill, takes pressure off the rupee, and brings down fuel costs across the economy.

Gold rose 1.19% to ₹1,54,980/10g. Investors often move into gold when uncertainty rises, making this move worth watching; silver similarly rose 3.87% to ₹2,58,252/kg.

The rupee strengthened to 92.95 against the dollar (-0.37%). A stronger rupee is good for importers but squeezes the margins of exporters like IT and pharma firms.

Options Activity

Options positioning: resistance at 25,000 (heaviest call OI), support at 23,000 (heaviest put OI). PCR at 1.03 is mildly bullish — more traders are writing puts, comfortable selling downside protection.

Nifty is above both the 21-EMA (23,742.72) and 50-EMA (24,286.02) — near and medium-term trends intact. A close below 23,742.72 would be the first warning.

VIX fell 4.86% to 17.2. In the 15–20 range — some nervousness, but not panic.
