Metals shine as Nifty edges higher — FIIs steady the ship for a third straight day

Market Overview

Nifty50 closed at 24,318.65, up 87 points or 0.36%, as FIIs continued their steady return with a net inflow of ₹666 Cr. The broader market kept pace, with the Sensex up 0.43% and midcap index climbing 0.57%. Breadth stayed decisively in favour of buyers: 5,953 advances against 2,682 declines. VIX eased further to 17.89, down 4.18%, signalling less anxiety as the relief rally that started Tuesday gains traction.

Broader Market

Nine of twelve sectors closed in green, with Nifty Metal leading the charge at 1.43% and IT adding 0.94%. Bank Nifty held steady at 0.43%, while smaller pockets of weakness showed up in Auto and Pharma, both down 0.22%. The mid-cap outperformance relative to Nifty suggests money is broadening its reach beyond the top fifty names. After three days of consistent FII buying since that Monday selloff, the tone has clearly shifted.

Institutional Flows

The rupee strengthened to 92.12, gaining 1.48% on the day, which likely helped metal prices and lifted gold and silver respectively by 0.54% and 0.94%. Crude edged down 0.52% but remains supported. A batch of economic data released yesterday, including WPI inflation, tenders data, and bank credit/deposit numbers, passed without disrupting the steady bid in equities. DIIs turned net sellers by ₹569 Cr, a flip from their heavy buying earlier in the week, suggesting domestic retail may be taking some profits after the bounce.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

The turnaround from Monday's washout has been clean and broad-based. Three days of FII inflows, all twelve sectors green yesterday, and nine green today paints a picture of restored confidence rather than a tactical bounce. Metal strength tied to rupee gains and steady domestic buying suggests some structural demand is holding. Stay alert for any fresh external shocks, but the path of least resistance is up.
