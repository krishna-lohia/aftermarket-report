Banking drags Nifty lower as metals stage rare rally, rupee strengthens sharply

Markets Today

Nifty dipped 0.38% to close at 24,138.25, extending the weakness that began yesterday. Bank Nifty bore the brunt with a 0.57% fall, while PSU Bank slid 0.6%. Sensex followed suit, dropping 0.42%. The real story was breadth, which tightened to near parity at 4,197 advances against 4,399 declines. Only 2 of 12 sectors finished green, a sharp reversal from yesterday's broad rally. VIX eased to 18.46, suggesting panic hasn't set in despite the selloff.

Broader Market

Midcap held its ground with a flat 0.03% gain, insulating itself from the largecap stumble. Metals stood out as the day's clear winner, with the Nifty Metal index rising 1.25% amid a broader commodity complex shift. IT added 0.58%, offering some diversification away from the troubled banking space. Auto fell the hardest at 0.73%, joining banks at the bottom. The narrow positive breadth hints that buyers are cherry-picking rather than committing fresh capital across the board.

FII / DII Flows

FIIs continued their measured buying, adding ₹666.15 Cr net for the second straight session. Domestics, though, trimmed holdings by ₹568.98 Cr. The rupee strengthened sharply by 1.48% to 92.12, a significant move that could ease import pressures and offer some relief to oil-dependent sectors. Crude slipped 0.36%, while gold and silver both rallied, with silver climbing 0.8%. Yesterday's economic data, including WPI and bank credit-deposit metrics, had already set the tone for caution around inflation and credit growth.

Watch for banking stabilization signals tomorrow, and keep an eye on how the rupee strength translates into corporate earnings momentum.
