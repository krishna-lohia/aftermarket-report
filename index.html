Banking weakness persists as Nifty slips past support — metals shine while breadth steadies

Market Overview

Nifty50 slipped 0.39% to close at 24,137.35, extending three sessions of churn that have left the index trapped between 24,000 and 24,250. The sell-off was mild but persistent. Banking took the hardest hit, with the Bank Nifty down 0.57% and PSU banks sinking 0.63%, dragging the broader index lower. FIIs stayed net buyers at ₹666 Cr, matching yesterday's inflow, which offered some stability even as domestic institutions trimmed positions by ₹569 Cr. VIX eased further to 18.46, reflecting calm rather than conviction.

Broader Market

Breadth narrowed sharply today. Advances outnumbered declines 4,360 to 4,250, a near-perfect split that signals a market stuck in the middle. The Midcap index managed a 0.07% gain, suggesting weakness is concentrated in large caps, particularly financials. Only two sectors closed in green, the fewest since Tuesday's broad rally. Metals led the charge with a 1.28% jump, while IT held up reasonably at +0.42%. Energy and FMCG both stayed flat or slightly negative, leaving little room for the index to build momentum.

Commodity Futures

A stronger rupee typically weighs on export earnings and can reduce the rupee value of foreign inflows, though FIIs haven't flinched so far this week.

Institutional Flows

Recent economic data released yesterday showed mixed signals that may be weighing on investor sentiment. WPI inflation ticked higher, while tenders awarded and bank credit growth painted a picture of an economy that's neither running hot nor cooling sharply. The unemployment rate stood at 4.9%, suggesting labour market stability, but deposit growth at 10.77% lags credit growth at 13.85%, a gap that could pressure bank margins if it widens further. This backdrop may explain why banking stocks are struggling even as FIIs continue to buy.

Change in OI for the day

The 24,000-24,200 zone continues to act as resistance. Nifty is now trading below its 50-day EMA at 24,280.66 but holding comfortably above the 21-day EMA at 23,675.53. Options positioning shows call buyers still targeting 25,000, while put support sits at 23,000, a 1,000-point band that's been the operational range for five sessions. The PCR ratio of 0.83 leans slightly bullish, suggesting more put buying than usual, but nothing extreme. Until we see a decisive break above 24,250 or below 24,000, expect more of this sideways grind.

Watch for any corporate earnings surprises or commentary about loan growth in the coming week.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

Today felt like a pause in what has been a volatile April. FIIs remain patient buyers, domestics are cautious sellers, and the index is firmly in a holding pattern. Banking needs to stabilise for the broader market to reignite. Metal strength is a bright spot, but it's not enough to drive the index higher when the largest sector by weight is under pressure. In this kind of neutral breadth, small positions and discipline beat conviction.
