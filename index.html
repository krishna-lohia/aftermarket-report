Banking slide drags Nifty lower as FIIs stay calm — metals keep the lights on

Market Overview

Nifty slipped 0.23% to close at 24,174.65, extending the pullback after three straight sessions of gains. The slide was sharp in banking, pharma and auto, while metals powered ahead with a 1.47% jump. Breadth stayed almost even at 4,411 advances versus 4,225 declines, a sign the selling wasn't panic-driven but sector-specific. VIX eased to 18.41, holding well below the 20 mark that signals genuine stress.

Institutional Flows

FIIs stayed buyers with ₹666 Cr net inflow, matching yesterday's number and holding steady even as banking and autos stumbled. This consistency matters — foreigners aren't rushing for exits, which would signal real trouble. DIIs flipped to net sellers, offloading ₹568.98 Cr, likely to lock in gains from the recent rally. The rupee strengthened by 1.48% to 92.12, helping imported inflation on crude, which fell 0.69%. Gold and silver rose 0.54% and 0.94% respectively, typical safe-haven moves when equity weakness spooks the market.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

The last three sessions tell a story of volatility within a contained range: brutal selling on the 13th, a full recovery on the 15th, and now a hesitant step backward. Banking's underperformance is worth tracking, but FII steady inflows and a calm VIX suggest conviction hasn't shifted to the downside. Metals leading suggests some conviction in specific pockets, and that breadth stayed balanced even in a down day. Watch if 24,100 holds as support or if tomorrow's ex-date moves trigger fresh positioning.
