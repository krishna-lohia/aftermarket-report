Nifty slips 0.86% to 23,842.65

Market Overview

Monday was a rough day for Indian stocks. Nifty 50, the benchmark index of India's 50 largest companies, closed at 23,842.65, fell 208 points (-0.86%) from the previous session.

The Sensex, which tracks 30 large BSE-listed companies and often moves in lockstep with Nifty, fell 0.91% to 76,847.57.

Bank Nifty moved broadly in line with the market, fell 0.55% to 55,605.05.

Market breadth, which tells you how many stocks fell versus rose, was poor today. 6,156 stocks declined against only 2,456 that advanced, out of 8,642 tracked. When more stocks fall than rise, even as the index holds up, it is worth paying attention. A 2.5:1 decline-to-advance ratio signals broad-based selling, not just weakness in a few index heavyweights.

Institutional Flows

Foreign institutional investors, known as FIIs, are the large global funds, hedge funds, and sovereign wealth funds that invest in Indian markets. They sold a net ₹1,983 Cr worth of Indian stocks today. This is a modest outflow, not alarming on its own, but worth tracking as part of the bigger picture.

Domestic institutional investors, or DIIs, are primarily Indian mutual funds, insurance companies, and pension funds. They bought ₹2,432 Cr today. DIIs absorbing FII selling is a key dynamic in the current market. Domestic money has increasingly become the floor that limits foreign-driven weakness.

Change in OI for the day

The options market is where traders place bets on where Nifty is headed, and reading it gives us a sense of where the so-called smart money expects resistance and support to emerge.

The largest concentration of call option sellers is at 23,850. Call sellers profit when Nifty stays below that level, so they have a financial incentive to defend it. Think of it as a ceiling for now. On the other side, the biggest cluster of put sellers is at 23,800. Put sellers profit when Nifty stays above it, so that is the floor they are defending.

The put-call ratio stands at 1.25, which is mildly bullish. PCR measures how many put options (downside bets and hedges) are open compared to calls (upside bets). A reading above 1 means more traders are writing puts, which is typically a bullish sign. It means they are comfortable selling downside protection.

On the chart, two levels matter most: the 21-day EMA (23,566.24) and the 50-day EMA (24,287.53). These are exponential moving averages — basically a smoothed-out average of recent closing prices that traders use as a gauge of trend direction.

Nifty is above its 21-day EMA at 23,566.24, which is a short-term positive. However, it is still below the 50-day EMA at 24,287.53, meaning the medium-term trend is still pointing down. A close above 24,287.53 would be the clearer signal that things are genuinely turning around.

India VIX, commonly called the fear index, closed at 20.50 today, up 8.75% from the previous session. VIX measures how much near-term volatility options traders are pricing in. A reading above 20 is elevated. It means the market is pricing in continued turbulence, and traders should expect larger-than-usual daily swings.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

For tomorrow, the key range to track on Nifty is 23,800 on the downside and 23,850 on the upside. Those are the levels where large option positions are concentrated, and they tend to act as natural anchors for price. A sustained break beyond either level could meaningfully accelerate the move in that direction.
