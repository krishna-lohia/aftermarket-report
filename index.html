Broad selloff grips markets as FIIs exit — only Energy holds ground

Market Overview

Nifty50 dropped 207.95 points to close at 23,842.65, down 0.86%, while Sensex fell 0.91%. The losses were broad and deep: just 2,456 stocks advanced against 6,156 declines. Only 1 out of 12 sectors closed in green. Volatility spiked with VIX jumping 8.75% to 20.5, signalling nervous sentiment as the market repriced.

Institutional Flows

FIIs turned aggressive sellers, pulling out 1,983.18 Cr net in a single session. That's part of a brutal five-day trend: FIIs have dumped 14,526.48 Cr since April 7, with only one relief rally on April 10. DIIs stepped in with 2,432.3 Cr of buying to cushion the blow, but domestic support isn't enough to stem the outflow tide. The rupee weakened 2.16% to 93.5 per dollar, a direct hit from FII selling pressure. Crude oil climbed 1.25% to 9,267, adding to imported inflation fears in the backdrop of today's CPI print.

Key Levels to Watch Tomorrow

Today's selloff is a reset after weeks of strength. The breadth collapse and sector rotation into defensive pockets (only Energy green, consumption stocks pummeled) marks a shift in risk appetite. Watch the 23,800 support level closely — a break below could accelerate downside as stop losses trigger. DIIs are fighting FIIs, but conviction has left the room.
